It is currently only compatible with a subset of the elements provided by the library which have a special structure, namely those where the basis can be described as a tensor product of one-dimensional polynomials. This opens for efficient transformation between vector entries and values or gradients in quadrature points with a technique that is called sum factorization. This technique has its origin in the spectral element community, started by the work of Orszag in 1980.
